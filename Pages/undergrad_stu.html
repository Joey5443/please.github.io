UnderGraduate Studies

Yale Under Grad Programs

Undergraduate applicants wishing to major in art at Yale must apply to Yale College directly.The program in art offers courses that provide part of a liberal education as well as preparation for professional work.

Please contact the Office of Undergraduate Admissions at PO Box 208234, 38 Hillhouse Avenue, New Haven CT 06520-8234.Phone: 1(203) 432-9300

Goals Of The Major

Students in this major will:

  • Develop an understanding of the visual arts through a studio-based curriculum
  • Apply fundamentals of art across a variety of media and disciplines
  • Relate the practice of making art to the fields of art history and theory
  • Gain a high level of mastery of at least one artistic discipline

Applying To Art Courses

In order to register for studio art courses, all of which require the permission of the instructor, students MUST attend the first class meeting.

Space is limited, and faculty make selections based on individual criteria, giving priority to MFA students, art majors, CPAR and intended art majors.

  • www.yalecollege.yale.edu.

Course Requirements

For graduation as an art major, a total of fourteen (14) course credits in the major field is required.

These fourteen course credits must include the following:

  1. Five prerequisite courses at the 100 level (including Visual Thinking and Basic Drawing)
  2. Four 200-level and above courses
  3. The Junior Major Seminar (Art 395a) or Critical Theory in the Studio (Art 201b)
  4. The Senior Project (Art 495 and 496)
  5. two courses in the History of Art.

A suggested program guideline is as follows:

  • Freshman year-Studio courses for 2 terms
  • Sophomore year-Studio courses for three terms including 1 Art History term
  • Junior year-Studio courses for 3 terms including the Junior Major Seminar includes Critical Theory
  • Senior year-Studio courses for four terms including the Senior Project.
